Ok, well now that we're on the same page(and becoming fast friends!)...

Hack Reactor is known to be intense and I want to be over-prepared. It is a marathon, but if I can run 2 or 3 miles at a 15 minute pace before the race even starts, then I might be able to take a few breaths.

My degree is in Chemical Engineering and though I took a CS class, I really don't remember it. Today, I am going to try to cram in some CS knowledge.

When I ask my CS friends what I should learn...they say: "algorithms and data structures". That's a starting point.

Let's break down how to approach these items:

  • Algorithms
    • Search
    • Sort
  • Data Structures
    • Singly and Doubly linked lists
    • Stack and Queue
    • Heap
    • Binary search trees
    • Graphs

I'm not sure what all of these are...so I am going to make another post with a learning plan. This post will hopefully capture any knowledge that I gain along the way!
