My journey to HR

21 May 2016

I think it's important to note that I did not pass the technical interview the first time...or the second time.

Getting into HR required a lot of studying. I did not remotely think that I would be this far along before even starting a bootcamp. It really seems like you need to be able to make use of all the free resources out there, and then HR will fill in the gaps.

I first applied in January 2016. I bombed the technical interview. I got so nervous and choked up. My mind went blank. It was not my best work.

So I went to studying. I studied higher order functions, HTML, CSS, jQuery, Node, Express...I went way overboard on non-essentials.

I decided I needed more guidance and entered Fulcrum. Fulcrum is amazing. I highly recommend it. Fulcrum helps you get up to speed on exactly what you need to know in regards to Javascript fundamentals. If you're serious about attending Hack Reactor, I can't recommend Fulcrum highly enough.

I prepared like crazy for the technical interview and memorized many many underscore functions. Bad idea. They care about problem solving, not memorization. I did not pass the second interview: I choked, I panicked, I blanked.

There's an underrated and under-appreciated skill that I had yet to develop: can you do technical work while someone else who has the answers judges you? Can you be a humble and vulnerable learner? Can you adapt to a teacher's corrections?

Practice this

What made me succeed in my third interview is repeated practice of technical interviews. I reached out to all of my dev friends and did skype as well as whiteboard interviews (that were above my skill level).

Thank you Conrad Wimberly and Landon Schropp, two of my practice interviewers!

Over time, I gradually became more comfortable with that type of coding. It's different, treat it as a separate skill.

Hope this helps!

Alex Stark
