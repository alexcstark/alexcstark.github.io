QuickSort

06 June 2016

I just figured out quicksort today. Let's see if I can make sense of it.

Quicksort is a sorting algorithm used to put items in order and is considered one of the most efficient ways of doing so (average run time O(n log n)).

For this example list, I'll describe how it works
9 0 8 7 3 2 1 5

  • You start with one of the values in the list. By convention, we pick the last number, "5". We call this our pivot.
    • The goal is now to put everything lower than the pivot to the left of it and everything higher than the pivot to the right of it. We do this in two partitions, the leftPartition and the rightPartition.
  • leftPartition will be 0 3 2 1
  • rightPartition will be 9 8 7
  • we now have 0 3 2 1 || 9 8 7 [5]
  • insert the 5 into the front-end of the rightpartition
  • 0 3 2 1 || 5 9 8 7
  • rinse and repeat for the two smaller partitions
